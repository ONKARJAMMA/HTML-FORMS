What is Open Source?

Open source refers to software that is released with a license granting anyone the freedom to view, use, modify, and distribute the source code. Unlike proprietary software, which is controlled and maintained by a single entity, open source software thrives on the collective effort of a diverse community of developers and enthusiasts.

At its core, open source embodies the principles of transparency, collaboration, and inclusivity. Unlike proprietary software, where the source code is restricted and controlled by a single entity, open source projects encourage a diverse range of contributors to collectively enhance, refine, and expand the software's capabilities. This democratization of development empowers individuals from around the world to contribute their unique skills, insights, and perspectives, resulting in software that is often more robust, secure, and adaptable.
